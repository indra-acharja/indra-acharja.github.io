Texas State University, San Marcos, TX

Thesis: Ungulate distribution, abundance, and habitat use in Phibsoo Wildlife Sanctuary, Bhutan.
Current Position
Doctoral Candidate & Research Assistant 2023–present
Department of Biology, Texas State University, San Marcos, TX
Conducting dissertation research integrating long-term field monitoring, nest camera technology, integrated population modelling, GIS-based threat assessment, and conservation breeding to generate the evidence base for recovery of the critically endangered White-bellied Heron.
Professional Experience
Chief Conservation Officer 2019–2023
Royal Society for Protection of Nature (RSPN), Thimphu, Bhutan
Led national White-bellied Heron recovery program. Established and managed the world's first captive population of the species at the White-bellied Heron Conservation Center. Directed multi-year population surveys, nest monitoring, and habitat assessment programs. Developed and implemented conservation breeding protocols. Coordinated over $7 million in conservation funding from international donors including MAVA Foundation, BMUV (Germany), Bhutan Trust Fund, and Hans Wilsdorf Foundation.
Project Officer 2014–2018
Royal Society for Protection of Nature (RSPN), Thimphu, Bhutan
Conducted annual population surveys, nest monitoring, and habitat assessments for the White-bellied Heron across Bhutan. Developed community-based conservation and education programs in White-bellied Heron habitats.
Research Interests
Avian ecology and demography · Freshwater ecology and biomonitoring · Breeding phenology and climate-driven ecological mismatch · Integrated population modelling · GIS-based landscape threat assessment · Conservation breeding and reintroduction biology · Global change biology · Long-term population monitoring
